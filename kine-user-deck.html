01 — Sound Familiar?

You show up every week.
So why does nobody build for you?

Follow a programme written for men and it's built around goals that aren't yours. Follow the beginner-friendly stuff and you outgrow it in weeks. Look into powerlifting and it's great — but it's more than most people are looking for. There's no middle ground — hardly any intelligent training built around how women actually want to look, feel and progress. 67% of women say the gym feels intimidating — not because of the weights, but because nobody showed them what to do with them.

The "smart" apps weren't built for you either. A dull screen of numbers you're not sure why you're recording. No sense of what to focus on, whether you're progressing, or what progress should even look like. Then life happens, you miss a week, and it feels like starting over — again. Women are 60% of fitness app users but stay 20% fewer days than men. The number one thing women want from a fitness app is to be taught — and almost none of them do it.

I spent ten years learning this the hard way. Programmes that weren't designed with me in mind — weighted crunches that gave me a wider waist I never asked for. A slipped disc because nobody told me the muscles I couldn't see mattered just as much as the ones I could. But I was getting smarter.

I ended up in powerlifting — and for the first time, training made sense. Real structure. Real education. Someone had thought about why. But I realised what I really wanted was the structure — and I hadn't found that anywhere else. You shouldn't need to commit to a sport just to train with intention, understand what you're doing, and see consistent progress. That's what Kinē was built for.
